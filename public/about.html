About Me

Hi, I’m Luke Devlin, an Applied Mathematics & Physics student with a practical streak for building things that make work easier. I’m especially interested in the crossroads of quantum science, data science, and engineering. Lately, my focus has been on creating lightweight AI agents and automations that search company servers, extract the right project information, and assemble clean technical submittals,freeing up time for deeper design work.

I’ve earned the IBM Basics of Quantum Information certificate and became a Qiskit Advocate, which pushed me to explain complex ideas clearly and mentor others. I enjoy turning messy, real-world requirements into tidy, repeatable workflows,whether that’s a Python script, an n8n pipeline, or a Word/Excel automation on Windows. On the data side, I’m building strong foundations in statistics, machine learning, and reproducible tooling so my projects are trustworthy and easy to maintain.

Beyond engineering, I like teaching (I’ve tutored maths and guitar) and sharing what I learn in accessible ways. I value clear structure, small iterative wins, and documentation that stands on its own. Over the next year, I’m growing my portfolio with hands-on projects across quantum, automation, and web,each one aimed at solving a real problem and showcasing solid engineering judgement.
